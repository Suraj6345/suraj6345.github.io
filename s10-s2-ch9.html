Chapter 9: Social Health

1

Define Social Health.

It is the ability of a person to establish purposeful relationships with others.
It involves the capacity to adapt to changing social situations.
A socially healthy person possesses qualities like tolerance and helpfulness.
It is not just the absence of conflict, but the presence of positive interaction.
Good social health contributes significantly to overall mental and physical well-being.
It reflects a person’s status and behavior within their community and family.
2

List the factors affecting Social Health.

Basic necessities like food, clothing, shelter, and medicines.
The educational and financial status of the individual and family.
The surrounding social and physical environment (neighborhood).
Availability of gardens, playgrounds, and recreational facilities.
Social safety and the prevailing law and order situation.
Access to transport, clean water, and proper sanitation.
3

What is the impact of Mental Stress on social health?

Increased competition in education and jobs creates significant pressure.
Nuclear families and working parents can lead to loneliness in children.
Stress can cause irritability, leading to social isolation and withdrawal.
Constant stress weakens the immune system, making the body prone to illness.
It can lead to bad habits like addiction as a coping mechanism.
Mental stress often results in poor communication with friends and family.
4

How does "Loneliness" affect teenagers today?

Many teenagers live in nuclear families where both parents work.
Lack of sibling interaction can lead to a sense of isolation.
Children may feel neglected or unable to share their feelings.
This loneliness often drives them towards excessive screen time.
It can lead to depression and a lack of social confidence.
In extreme cases, it makes them vulnerable to negative peer pressure.
5

What are the dangers of Addiction?

Peer pressure is a major reason for teenagers to start using tobacco or alcohol.
Substances like nicotine and alcohol are highly addictive and habit-forming.
Addiction leads to the degradation of physical health and vital organs.
It causes financial loss and destroys the reputation of the family.
Addicts often lose their ability to think rationally and behave socially.
Long-term addiction to tobacco can cause various types of cancer.
6

Explain the effects of Tobacco/Nicotine.

Nicotine is the primary addictive substance found in tobacco products.
It causes the narrowing of blood vessels, leading to heart diseases.
Tobacco chewing is a direct cause of oral (mouth) and throat cancer.
Smoking damages the lungs and can lead to chronic respiratory issues.
It affects the nervous system, leading to tremors and lack of focus.
It also reduces the oxygen-carrying capacity of the blood.
7

How does Alcoholism affect an individual?

Alcohol directly affects the central nervous system, slowing down reactions.
Chronic consumption leads to the failure of the liver (cirrhosis).
It results in the loss of memory and decreased learning capacity.
An alcoholic person loses control over their speech and body movements.
It often leads to domestic violence and social embarrassment.
The lifespan of a chronic alcoholic is significantly shortened.
8

What is "Incurable Disease" stress?

Diseases like AIDS, TB, or cancer cause long-term mental trauma.
Patients often face social stigma and rejection from society.
The high cost of treatment creates a massive financial burden.
Families often experience "Caregiver Burnout" while helping the patient.
Chronic illness can lead to a sense of hopelessness and depression.
Knowledge of such diseases is essential to treat patients with empathy.
9

Explain the impact of Excessive Use of Modern Technology.

Spending hours on mobile phones leads to "Physical Fatigue."
It causes ailments like headaches, insomnia, and neck pain.
Excessive screen time reduces the time spent on physical exercise.
It can lead to "Digital Addiction," where a person cannot live without devices.
Technology often replaces real-life social interactions with virtual ones.
It can lead to psychological issues like anxiety and blurred vision.
10

What is "Selfie-cide"?

It refers to accidental deaths occurring while trying to take a selfie.
People often ignore risks like deep water, heights, or moving trains.
This behavior shows a lack of awareness of one’s surroundings.
It is driven by the desire for social media validation and "likes."
Governments have marked many spots as "No Selfie Zones" for safety.
It is a modern manifestation of risky and impulsive behavior.
11

What is the "Social Media" effect on the brain?

Receiving notifications triggers the release of dopamine in the brain.
This creates a cycle of addiction where the user craves more interaction.
It can lead to "FOMO" (Fear Of Missing Out) among teenagers.
Users often compare their lives with the filtered lives of others.
This comparison leads to low self-esteem and body image issues.
Prolonged use reduces the attention span and ability to concentrate.
12

Define Cybercrime.

It is any criminal activity that involves a computer or a network.
It includes hacking, identity theft, and online financial fraud.
Spreading fake news or hate speech on social media is a cybercrime.
Using someone else’s photos or identity without permission is illegal.
Hacking government or bank websites is a severe offense under the law.
Cybercrimes are governed by the Information Technology (IT) Act.
13

List common types of Cybercrimes.

Phishing: Using fake emails to steal bank details or passwords.
Cyberstalking: Harassing or following someone online repeatedly.
Identity Theft: Stealing personal info to commit fraud in their name.
Hacking: Gaining unauthorized access to a private computer system.
Cyberbullying: Using the internet to mock, threaten, or shame others.
Piracy: Distributing copyrighted movies or software without a license.
14

What are the signs of Cyberbullying?

Receiving threatening or offensive messages on social media.
Spreading rumors or embarrassing photos of a person online.
Excluding someone intentionally from online groups or activities.
Creating fake profiles to impersonate and ruin someone's reputation.
Victims often become quiet, anxious, or stop using the internet.
It can lead to extreme mental stress and suicidal thoughts in children.
15

How to protect yourself from Cybercrime?

Never share your bank passwords or OTPs with anyone on the phone.
Use strong, unique passwords for all your online accounts.
Avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading unknown attachments.
Do not share personal photos or home addresses with strangers online.
Keep your computer and mobile software updated with the latest security.
Always report cybercrime immediately to the nearest police station.
16

What is the "IT Act 2000"?

It is the primary law in India dealing with cybercrime and e-commerce.
It was enacted on 17th October 2000 to provide legal framework.
It prescribes punishments for hacking, data theft, and online obscenity.
The Act was amended in 2008 to include more specific cyber offenses.
It empowers the police to investigate crimes committed using the internet.
It has made digital signatures and electronic records legally valid.
17

Explain "Stress Management" through communication.

Expressing your feelings to friends or family reduces mental pressure.
Talking to a trusted person helps in gaining a new perspective.
Writing a diary or journal is a good way to vent out emotions.
Communication prevents the buildup of negative thoughts in the mind.
Seeking advice from teachers or counselors is highly beneficial.
Keeping feelings bottled up inside can lead to chronic depression.
18

What is the importance of Hobbies in social health?

Hobbies like painting, singing, or dancing act as "Stress Busters."
They divert the mind from daily pressures and create a sense of joy.
Developing a hobby increases a person’s self-confidence.
Activities like photography or bird watching connect us with nature.
They provide an opportunity to meet people with similar interests.
Utilizing free time for a hobby prevents the mind from being idle.
19

How does Laughter help in managing stress?

Laughter triggers the release of endorphins, the "feel-good" chemicals.
It reduces the level of stress hormones like cortisol in the body.
Many people join "Laughter Clubs" to practice laughing together.
It improves blood circulation and boosts the immune system.
Laughter helps in lightening the mood in tense social situations.
It is one of the simplest and most effective ways to stay healthy.
20

What is the role of Sports/Physical Exercise?

Physical activity helps in burning calories and staying fit.
It teaches qualities like teamwork, discipline, and sportsmanship.
Playing outdoors reduces the time spent on addictive mobile phones.
Exercise improves the concentration and focus of students.
It provides a natural outlet for anger and frustration.
Regular sports activities build resilience and mental strength.
21

Explain "Yoga and Meditation".

Yoga involves physical postures (asanas) and breathing exercises.
It improves the flexibility of the body and calms the nervous system.
Meditation helps in achieving "Mindfulness" and mental peace.
It is very effective in reducing anxiety and improving sleep.
Regular practice increases the power of memory and concentration.
It is an ancient Indian technique now recognized worldwide.
22

What are the symptoms of "Digital Addiction"?

Feeling restless or irritable when the phone is taken away.
Checking the phone immediately after waking up and before sleeping.
Losing track of time while browsing social media or gaming.
Neglecting school work, family, or physical hygiene for devices.
Experiencing phantom vibrations (feeling the phone ring when it isn't).
Preferring online chatting over real-life face-to-face conversations.
23

How do "Laughter Clubs" work?

People gather in parks or community centers early in the morning.
They perform synchronized loud laughter exercises as a group.
It often includes clapping and simple stretching movements.
It helps in relieving the stress accumulated throughout the day.
It creates a sense of community and social belonging among members.
This practice is scientifically proven to improve mental well-being.
24

What is the "Salaam Mumbai Foundation"?

It is an NGO that works to make schools and societies tobacco-free.
It conducts awareness programs in urban and rural areas of Mumbai.
They educate children about the dangers of tobacco and smoking.
They provide support to those who want to quit their addiction.
The foundation has helped thousands of schools become "Tobacco Free."
It plays a major role in improving the social health of the youth.
25

Mention some "Helpline" services for social health.

Childline (1098) provides help for children in distress or danger.
Senior Citizen Helplines assist the elderly with safety and health.
Suicide prevention lifelines offer counseling to those in despair.
Cybercrime portals allow victims to report online fraud safely.
Women's helplines provide support against domestic violence.
These services provide immediate, confidential, and free assistance.
26

What is the relationship between Education and Social Health?

Education provides the knowledge to distinguish between right and wrong.
It creates awareness about social issues and health hazards.
Educated individuals are less likely to fall into the trap of addiction.
It improves communication skills and social interaction.
Education opens up better job opportunities, reducing financial stress.
A literate society has a much higher level of social health.
27

How does "Peer Pressure" work?

It is the influence exerted by a group on an individual to follow them.
Positive peer pressure encourages studying or playing sports.
Negative peer pressure often leads to smoking, drinking, or bunking.
Teenagers often succumb to it because of the need to "fit in."
It takes a strong character and correct guidance to resist it.
Parents should be aware of the friends their children hang out with.
28

What are the ill effects of "Alcohol" on the liver?

The liver is the main organ responsible for detoxifying alcohol.
Excessive drinking causes fat to accumulate in the liver (Fatty Liver).
Over time, this leads to inflammation called Alcoholic Hepatitis.
The final stage is "Cirrhosis," where liver tissue is permanently scarred.
A scarred liver cannot filter toxins from the blood efficiently.
Liver failure is often fatal unless a transplant is performed.
29

What is "Phubbing"?

It is a modern term for "Phone Snubbing."
It involves ignoring a person you are with to look at your phone.
It is a sign of poor social etiquette and digital addiction.
Phubbing can damage relationships and create feelings of rejection.
It reduces the quality of face-to-face social interaction.
Awareness of this behavior is necessary to maintain social health.
30

Explain "Mental Illness" and its social perception.

Mental illness includes depression, bipolar disorder, and schizophrenia.
Unlike physical illness, mental illness is often hidden by patients.
There is a lot of "Stigma" and shame attached to it in society.
People often mock or fear those who have mental health issues.
This prevents patients from seeking the medical help they need.
Compassion and professional counseling are the keys to recovery.
31

What are "Life Skills"?

These are abilities for adaptive and positive behavior.
They include critical thinking, problem-solving, and empathy.
Life skills help individuals deal with the demands of everyday life.
"Resilience" is a life skill that helps us bounce back from failure.
Schools are now including life skills training in their curriculum.
These skills are essential for maintaining long-term social health.
32

What is the impact of "Nuclear Families" on social health?

In nuclear families, there are fewer family members to interact with.
Children often miss out on the guidance of grandparents.
It can lead to a sense of isolation if both parents are busy.
However, it can also lead to more focused attention on the child.
The burden of domestic work is often higher in nuclear families.
Social health depends on how well the family maintains outside bonds.
33

How to help a friend who is depressed?

Listen to them patiently without judging or giving immediate advice.
Encourage them to talk about their feelings and fears.
Spend time with them doing simple activities like walking or gaming.
Remind them that they are loved and that their life matters.
Gently suggest that they speak to a professional counselor or teacher.
Never leave them alone if they mention self-harm or suicide.

What are the signs of "Mobile Addiction" in children?

Constant checking for messages even when there is no notification.
Poor academic performance due to excessive gaming or browsing.
Lack of interest in outdoor games or physical activities.
Becoming aggressive when the internet connection is slow.
Hiding the phone use from parents or lying about it.
Eye strain, blurred vision, and frequent headaches.
35

What is "E-waste" and social responsibility?

E-waste refers to discarded electronic devices like phones and TVs.
It contains toxic substances like Lead, Mercury, and Cadmium.
Improper disposal can pollute the soil and groundwater.
Social health involves taking responsibility for the environment.
We should always give our old electronics to certified recyclers.
Reducing the frequency of buying new devices helps in reducing waste.
36

Explain "Cyber Hygiene".

It refers to the practices used to maintain a healthy online life.
Regularly changing passwords and using two-factor authentication.
Being mindful of what you post on social media (Digital Footprint).
Not engaging with trolls or strangers who ask for personal info.
Verifying the information before sharing it with others (fake news).
Limiting the time spent on the internet to avoid addiction.
37

How does "Poor Nutrition" affect social health?

Junk food lacks the vitamins and minerals needed for brain health.
It can lead to obesity, which might cause low self-esteem.
Nutritional deficiencies make a person tired and irritable.
A healthy diet improves mood and energy levels for socializing.
Food habits are often a part of social gatherings and culture.
Teaching children about balanced diets is vital for their future.
38

What is "First Aid" for mental health?

It is the initial support given to a person experiencing a crisis.
Assess the risk of harm and offer a non-judgmental listening ear.
Provide reassurance and information about where to get help.
Encourage the person to reach out to professional support.
Help them connect with their friends or family for comfort.
Mental health first aid can save lives in critical situations.
39

What is the "Digital Footprint"?

It is the trail of data you leave behind while using the internet.
This includes social media posts, comments, and search history.
Once something is posted online, it is very difficult to delete it.
Future employers often check a person’s digital footprint.
A negative footprint can ruin your reputation and social health.
We must always think twice before posting anything online.
40

Why are "Playgrounds" disappearing?

Rapid urbanization and construction are taking over open spaces.
This reduces the opportunities for children to play outdoor sports.
Lack of play areas leads to sedentary lifestyles and obesity.
It forces children to stay indoors and use digital devices.
Governments must ensure every locality has a dedicated park.
Playgrounds are essential for the social development of children.
41

What is "Phishing" in detail?

It is a fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information like passwords.
Hackers send emails that look like they are from a bank or Google.
These emails ask the user to "Update account info" by clicking a link.
The link leads to a fake website that records your login details.
Banks never ask for your password or OTP via email or SMS.
Always check the URL of the website before entering sensitive data.
42

What is "Identity Theft"?

It involves using someone else's personal data without their consent.
Criminals use stolen info to open bank accounts or get credit cards.
It can be done through social media hacking or stealing ID cards.
The victim may be held responsible for the crimes committed in their name.
It causes immense mental stress and financial loss to the victim.
Protecting your Aadhaar and Pan card details is very important.
43

Explain "Cyberstalking".

It is the use of the internet to stalk or harass an individual.
The stalker follows the victim's every move on social media.
They may send threatening messages or post defamatory comments.
It creates a sense of fear and insecurity in the victim's mind.
It is a punishable offense under the IT Act of India.
Blocking the stalker and reporting to the police is the right step.

What are "Hacking" penalties in India?

Hacking is gaining unauthorized access to computer resources.
Under Section 66 of the IT Act, it is a punishable crime.
It can lead to imprisonment for up to 3 years.
A heavy fine of up to 5 lakh rupees can also be imposed.
Stealing or destroying data is also covered under these laws.
Intentional damage to government systems carries even harsher penalties.

How to identify "Fake News" online?

Check the source of the information and the website's URL.
Look for the same news on reputable mainstream news channels.
Pay attention to the date and time of the post (it might be old).
Beware of headlines that are designed to make you angry or scared.
Reverse search the images to see if they are from a different event.
Never share news unless you are 100% sure that it is true.
