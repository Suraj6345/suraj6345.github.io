Algebra: Practice Set 1.1

Q.1 Complete the activity to solve simultaneous equations.
\(5x + 3y = 9\) --- (I)
\(2x - 3y = 12\) --- (II)
Adding equations (I) and (II):
$$\begin{aligned} 5x + 3y &= 9 \\ + \quad 2x - 3y &= 12 \\ \hline 7x &= 21 \\ x &= \frac{21}{7} \\ x &= 3 \end{aligned}$$
Place \(x = 3\) in equation (I).
\(5 \times 3 + 3y = 9\)
\(15 + 3y = 9\)
\(3y = 9 - 15 \implies 3y = -6\)
\(y = \frac{-6}{3} \implies y = -2\)
Solution is \((x, y) = (3, -2)\).
Q.2 (1) Solve: \(3a + 5b = 26; \quad a + 5b = 22\)
Subtracting the second equation from the first:
$$\begin{aligned} 3a + 5b &= 26 \\ - \quad (a + 5b &= 22) \\ \hline 2a &= 4 \\ a &= 2 \end{aligned}$$
Substitute \(a = 2\) in \(a + 5b = 22\):
\(2 + 5b = 22 \implies 5b = 20 \implies b = 4\)
Solution: \((a, b) = (2, 4)\).
Q.2 (2) Solve: \(x + 7y = 10; \quad 3x - 2y = 7\)
Multiply equation (I) by 3: \(3x + 21y = 30\)
Subtract equation (II) from this:
$$\begin{aligned} 3x + 21y &= 30 \\ - \quad (3x - 2y &= 7) \\ \hline 23y &= 23 \\ y &= 1 \end{aligned}$$
Substitute \(y = 1\) in \(x + 7y = 10\):
\(x + 7(1) = 10 \implies x = 3\)
Solution: \((x, y) = (3, 1)\).
Q.2 (3) Solve: \(2x - 3y = 9; \quad 2x + y = 13\)
Subtracting equation (I) from (II):
$$\begin{aligned} 2x + y &= 13 \\ - \quad (2x - 3y &= 9) \\ \hline 4y &= 4 \\ y &= 1 \end{aligned}$$
Substitute \(y = 1\) in \(2x + y = 13\):
\(2x + 1 = 13 \implies 2x = 12 \implies x = 6\)
Solution: \((x, y) = (6, 1)\).
Q.2 (4) Solve: \(5m - 3n = 19; \quad m - 6n = -7\)
Multiply second equation by 5: \(5m - 30n = -35\)
Subtract from first equation:
$$\begin{aligned} 5m - 3n &= 19 \\ - \quad (5m - 30n &= -35) \\ \hline 27n &= 54 \\ n &= 2 \end{aligned}$$
Substitute \(n = 2\) in \(m - 6n = -7\):
\(m - 6(2) = -7 \implies m - 12 = -7 \implies m = 5\)
Solution: \((m, n) = (5, 2)\).
Q.2 (5) Solve: \(5x + 2y = -3; \quad x + 5y = 4\)
Multiply second equation by 5: \(5x + 25y = 20\)
Subtract first equation:
$$\begin{aligned} 5x + 25y &= 20 \\ - \quad (5x + 2y &= -3) \\ \hline 23y &= 23 \\ y &= 1 \end{aligned}$$
Substitute \(y = 1\) in \(x + 5y = 4\):
\(x + 5(1) = 4 \implies x = -1\)
Solution: \((x, y) = (-1, 1)\).
Q.2 (6) Solve: \(\frac{1}{3}x + y = \frac{10}{3}; \quad 2x + \frac{1}{4}y = \frac{11}{4}\)
Multiply first by 3: \(x + 3y = 10\) ---(A)
Multiply second by 4: \(8x + y = 11\) ---(B)
Multiply (B) by 3: \(24x + 3y = 33\)
Subtract (A):
$$\begin{aligned} 24x + 3y &= 33 \\ - \quad (x + 3y &= 10) \\ \hline 23x &= 23 \\ x &= 1 \end{aligned}$$
Substitute \(x = 1\) in (A): \(1 + 3y = 10 \implies y = 3\)
Solution: \((x, y) = (1, 3)\).
Q.2 (7) Solve: \(99x + 101y = 499; \quad 101x + 99y = 501\)
Adding equations:
$$\begin{aligned} 99x + 101y &= 499 \\ + \quad 101x + 99y &= 501 \\ \hline 200x + 200y &= 1000 \\ x + y &= 5 \quad \text{---(III)} \end{aligned}$$
Subtracting (I) from (II):
$$\begin{aligned} 101x + 99y &= 501 \\ - \quad (99x + 101y &= 499) \\ \hline 2x - 2y &= 2 \\ x - y &= 1 \quad \text{---(IV)} \end{aligned}$$
Adding (III) and (IV):
$$\begin{aligned} x + y &= 5 \\ + \quad x - y &= 1 \\ \hline 2x &= 6 \implies x = 3 \end{aligned}$$
Solution: \((x, y) = (3, 2)\).
Q.2 (8) Solve: \(49x - 57y = 172; \quad 57x - 49y = 252\)
Adding equations:
$$\begin{aligned} 49x - 57y &= 172 \\ + \quad 57x - 49y &= 252 \\ \hline 106x - 106y &= 424 \\ x - y &= 4 \quad \text{---(III)} \end{aligned}$$
Subtracting (I) from (II):
$$\begin{aligned} 57x - 49y &= 252 \\ - \quad (49x - 57y &= 172) \\ \hline 8x + 8y &= 80 \\ x + y &= 10 \quad \text{---(IV)} \end{aligned}$$
Adding (III) and (IV):
$$\begin{aligned} x - y &= 4 \\ + \quad x + y &= 10 \\ \hline 2x &= 14 \implies x = 7 \end{aligned}$$
Solution: \((x, y) = (7, 3)\).
