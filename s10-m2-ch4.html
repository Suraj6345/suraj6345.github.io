Geometric Constructions

Q1.

Construction of Tangents to a circle.

  • At a point on the circle: Use the radius and draw a perpendicular line at the point of contact.
  • From a point outside the circle: Draw a segment joining the center and the external point. Construct its perpendicular bisector to find the midpoint, draw a circle/arc, and connect the intersection points.
Q2.

Construction of Similar Triangles.

When constructing $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle PQR$ with a given ratio (e.g., 3:4):

  • If the triangles share a common vertex, use the "ray method" to divide the segment into equal parts.
  • If no common vertex, calculate the lengths of the second triangle using the ratio of corresponding sides.
