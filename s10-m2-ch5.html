Co-ordinate Geometry

Q1.

State the Distance Formula.

The distance between points $A(x_1, y_1)$ and $B(x_2, y_2)$ is:

$d(A,B) = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$

Q2.

Explain Section Formula and Midpoint Formula.

  • Section Formula: For a point dividing a segment in ratio $m:n$:
    $x = \frac{mx_2 + nx_1}{m+n}, y = \frac{my_2 + ny_1}{m+n}$
  • Midpoint Formula: $x = \frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, y = \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2}$
  • Centroid Formula: $G = (\frac{x_1+x_2+x_3}{3}, \frac{y_1+y_2+y_3}{3})$
